Top Home Security Solutions in Kenya 2026: Complete Guide

April 14, 2026
9 min read
Kim Electronics Team

Home security in Kenya has evolved dramatically over the last decade. What was once available only to wealthy estates, professional CCTV, electric fencing, smart locks, and integrated alarm systems, is now accessible to middle-income homeowners at surprisingly affordable prices. In this comprehensive guide, we walk through every home security solution available in Kenya today, helping you make informed decisions about protecting your family and property.

Why Home Security Matters More Than Ever in Kenya

Kenya's urban areas, particularly Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, and Nakuru, have seen steady growth in residential crime over recent years. Beyond simple theft and burglary, home invasion and carjacking remain concerns for many families. The good news is that modern security systems have proven highly effective:

  • Homes with visible CCTV cameras are 60% less likely to be targeted by burglars
  • Electric fences successfully deter over 80% of perimeter breach attempts
  • Homes with alarm systems have a 300% lower burglary rate than unprotected homes
  • Smart home security allows real-time monitoring from anywhere in the world

Layer 1: Perimeter Security

The first and most important layer of home security is the perimeter, ensuring intruders cannot easily enter your compound in the first place.

Electric Fence

The gold standard for perimeter security in Kenya. An electric fence delivers a powerful but non-lethal shock to anyone who attempts to climb or cut through it. When connected to an alarm system, any breach triggers an immediate alert. Read our full electric fence guide.

Best for: Standalone houses, gated compounds, farms, and commercial properties.
Cost: KSh 1,200–2,000 per running metre installed.

Automated Gates with Intercom

An automatic gate with a video intercom system allows you to see and speak to anyone at your gate before granting access, all from your phone, even when you're away from home. Combined with a video doorbell camera, you have complete control over who enters your property.

Best for: Any property with a gate, homes, apartments, offices.
Cost: Gate automation from KSh 25,000; video intercom from KSh 10,000.

Layer 2: Surveillance (CCTV)

Once perimeter security is in place, CCTV cameras form the second layer, recording everything that happens within and around your property and sending alerts when motion is detected.

Recommended Home CCTV Setup

For a typical 3–4 bedroom Nairobi home, we recommend:

  • 2–4 outdoor cameras covering entry points, gate, driveway, and perimeter
  • 1–2 indoor cameras at main entry points (front door, living room)
  • 8-channel NVR with 1–2TB hard drive (30+ days of recording)
  • Night vision cameras (colour night vision preferred)
  • Remote viewing app configured on all family smartphones

See our guide to the best CCTV cameras for Kenya.

Layer 3: Intrusion Detection (Alarm System)

Even if someone bypasses the perimeter and CCTV, an alarm system provides immediate detection and alert when an intruder enters your home.

A modern home alarm system includes:

  • PIR (Passive Infrared) motion detectors in key rooms
  • Magnetic sensors on all doors and windows
  • A control panel with user codes
  • A loud outdoor siren to alert neighbours
  • A GSM dialler that sends SMS and calls your phone when triggered
  • Optional: monitoring by a security company (armed response)

Cost: Basic home alarm from KSh 12,000 installed.

Layer 4: Access Control

Access control systems restrict who can enter specific areas of your property. For homes, this typically means:

  • Smart locks: Replace your standard door lock with a smart lock that can be opened by fingerprint, PIN, RFID card, or smartphone app. You can grant and revoke access remotely, and see a log of who entered and when.
  • Electric strike locks: An electromagnetic lock on your gate or door that releases only when authorised, integrated with your intercom system.

Cost: Smart door locks from KSh 8,000; electric strike locks from KSh 5,000.

Layer 5: Smart Home Integration

The newest frontier in home security is smart home automation, integrating all your security systems (CCTV, alarm, access control, electric fence) into a single platform controlled from your smartphone.

Modern smart home security systems allow you to:

  • Arm/disarm your alarm from anywhere via app
  • View all CCTV cameras simultaneously on one screen
  • Control your gate and locks remotely
  • Set automatic routines (e.g., arm alarm when everyone leaves, turn on outdoor lights at sunset)
  • Receive real-time push notifications for any security events
  • Integrate with voice assistants (Google Home, Amazon Alexa)

Choosing the Right Security Company in Kenya

Not all security installers are equal. When hiring a company to secure your home, look for:

  • ERC license: Only hire ERC-licensed contractors for electrical and alarm work
  • Brand certifications: Hikvision, Dahua, and Uniview certified installers use genuine equipment correctly
  • Written quote: Get everything in writing, equipment spec, quantities, and price, before work begins
  • Warranty: Any reputable installer offers at least 12 months workmanship warranty
  • References and reviews: Check Google reviews and ask for references from completed projects
