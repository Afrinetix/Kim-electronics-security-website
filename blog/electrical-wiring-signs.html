7 Warning Signs Your Home Electrical Wiring Needs Replacement

April 22, 2026
6 min read
Kim Electronics Team

Faulty electrical wiring is one of the leading causes of house fires in Kenya, responsible for hundreds of residential fires every year. Many of these tragedies are preventable, if homeowners knew the warning signs that indicate their wiring has deteriorated or was poorly installed in the first place.

In this guide, our ERC-licensed electricians outline the seven most critical warning signs that mean you should call a qualified electrician immediately, and what the risks of ignoring them are.

Important Safety Warning

If you experience any of the warning signs in this article, especially burning smells, sparks, or frequent tripping, do not ignore them. Turn off the main switch and call a licensed electrician immediately. Electrical fires can spread rapidly and silently within wall cavities.

Sign #1: Frequent Circuit Breaker Tripping

Circuit breakers are designed to trip when a circuit draws more current than it can safely handle, protecting your home from overheating and fire. Occasional tripping is normal. But if you're resetting your breaker weekly or even daily, this is a serious warning sign.

Common causes of frequent tripping include: overloaded circuits (too many appliances on one circuit), undersized wiring for modern appliances, faulty breakers, or a short circuit somewhere in the wiring. A licensed electrician needs to investigate and diagnose the root cause, don't just reset the breaker and ignore it.

Sign #2: Burning Smell or Scorch Marks

Any unexplained burning smell, especially near sockets, switches, or the distribution board, is an emergency. Overheating wiring has a distinctive, acrid plastic smell. Scorch marks or discolouration on outlets, switches, or wall surfaces near electrical fittings indicate that arcing (electrical sparking inside the wall) has already occurred.

What to do: Turn off the main switch immediately. Do not use the affected outlets. Call an electrician, do not sleep in the house until the fault is identified and repaired.

Sign #3: Flickering or Dimming Lights

Lights that flicker occasionally when a heavy appliance (like a washing machine or microwave) starts up is relatively normal, these appliances draw a large current on startup. However, lights that constantly flicker, dim without explanation, or flicker in multiple rooms simultaneously often indicate:

  • Loose or corroded wiring connections
  • Overloaded circuits shared between lighting and heavy appliances
  • Deteriorating wiring insulation causing intermittent shorts
  • A poor connection at the main service entry point

Sign #4: Warm or Discoloured Outlets and Switches

Electrical outlets and switches should never feel warm to the touch under normal circumstances. A warm outlet or switch face plate indicates excessive current draw, a loose connection, or overheated wiring behind it. Discolouration, yellowing, browning, or blackening, means heat damage has already occurred.

This is particularly common in older Kenyan homes where a single socket was installed and multiple adaptors/extension leads have been used to plug in many appliances simultaneously, far exceeding the circuit's designed load.

Sign #5: Sparks When Plugging In Appliances

Seeing a small, brief blue spark when plugging in an appliance is relatively normal, it's caused by the initial surge of current as the circuit connects. However, if you see large, persistent, or yellow sparks, especially accompanied by a popping sound, this indicates a serious wiring fault that needs immediate attention.

Persistent sparking can cause arcing faults that ignite building materials inside walls and ceilings. Arcing fires often start inside walls and burn for hours before smoke becomes visible, by which point the structural damage can be catastrophic.

Sign #6: Old or Outdated Wiring Types

If your home was built before the 1990s and has never been rewired, there's a good chance it has one of these outdated wiring types:

  • Aluminium wiring: Used in the 1970s–80s, aluminium wiring expands and contracts differently from copper, causing loose connections over time. It also oxidises, increasing resistance and fire risk.
  • Rubber-insulated wiring: Old rubber insulation becomes brittle and cracks with age, exposing live conductors.
  • Undersized wiring: Homes wired decades ago were designed for far fewer electrical appliances than a modern household uses. Undersized cables overheat when carrying modern loads.

If you're unsure what type of wiring your home has, an electrician can inspect it quickly and advise whether rewiring is recommended.

Sign #7: No Safety Earth (Ground) Connection

Modern electrical installations require a proper earth (ground) connection, a safety wire that provides a path for fault current to flow safely to the ground rather than through you. Many older Kenyan homes were wired without an earth connection, or with an inadequate earthing system.

Signs of no earth connection include: getting mild shocks when touching metal appliances, no earth pin hole in sockets, or a licensed electrician confirming poor earthing during an inspection. Without proper earthing, a simple appliance fault can be lethal.

What to Do If You Notice These Signs

If you've identified any of these warning signs in your home:

  1. For immediate dangers (burning smell, sparks, scorch marks), turn off the main switch and call us immediately
  2. For non-urgent signs, book an electrical inspection with a licensed electrician
  3. Get a written report of any faults found and a quote for repairs
  4. Ensure any work is done by an ERC-licensed contractor who can provide a compliance certificate
