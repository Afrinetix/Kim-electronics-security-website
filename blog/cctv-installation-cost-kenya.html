How Much Does CCTV Installation Cost in Kenya? 2026 Complete Price Guide

April 5, 2026
7 min read
Kim Electronics Team

One of the most common questions we receive is "How much does CCTV cost in Kenya?" The answer is, it depends. CCTV installation costs vary significantly based on the number of cameras, camera quality, recording system, cable distances, and the installer you choose. In this honest guide, we break down exactly what drives the cost of a CCTV system in Kenya, typical price ranges for different system sizes, and how to avoid overpaying or getting a substandard installation.

Updated for 2026

All prices in this guide are in Kenya Shillings (KSh) and reflect current market rates as of May 2026. Prices include supply of genuine equipment and professional installation unless otherwise noted.

What Affects the Cost of CCTV Installation in Kenya?

1. Number of Cameras

The most obvious cost driver. Each additional camera adds the camera cost, cabling, connectors, conduit, and labour. Approximate cost per additional camera (Hikvision 4MP IP): KSh 8,000–15,000 including installation.

2. Camera Quality and Resolution

There's a significant cost difference between camera tiers:

  • 2MP (1080p) basic IP cameras: KSh 3,500–6,000 per camera (supply only)
  • 4MP genuine Hikvision/Dahua: KSh 6,500–12,000 per camera
  • 8MP (4K) cameras: KSh 10,000–20,000 per camera
  • AI/Smart detection cameras: KSh 12,000–35,000 per camera
  • PTZ cameras: KSh 35,000–120,000 per camera

3. Recording System (DVR / NVR)

The NVR or DVR is the heart of the system, it records, stores, and manages camera feeds:

  • 4-channel NVR (Hikvision/Dahua): KSh 8,000–15,000
  • 8-channel NVR: KSh 12,000–22,000
  • 16-channel NVR: KSh 18,000–35,000
  • 32-channel NVR: KSh 30,000–60,000

4. Hard Drive (Storage)

Storage is often underpriced in cheap quotes, or even omitted entirely. Recommended storage for different systems:

  • 4 cameras, 30 days recording: 1TB hard drive (KSh 7,000–10,000)
  • 8 cameras, 30 days: 2TB hard drive (KSh 11,000–16,000)
  • 16 cameras, 30 days: 4TB hard drive (KSh 18,000–25,000)

Recording duration also depends on resolution settings and whether motion-only recording is enabled. Ask your installer exactly how many days of recording the quoted storage provides.

5. Cabling and Installation Labour

Labour and cabling typically accounts for 30–50% of the total installation cost. Factors affecting this cost include:

  • Distance from each camera to the NVR (longer runs = more cable)
  • Whether conduit/trunking is required for a neat installation
  • Difficulty of cable routing (concrete walls, ceiling voids, outdoor runs)
  • Height of camera installation (tall buildings require scaffolding or ladders)

Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

Some installers quote a low headline price but add charges later. Common extras that should ideally be included in the quoted price:

  • Power supply units and UPS (uninterruptible power supply)
  • Cable conduit, trunking, and wall plugs
  • BNC connectors, RJ45 connectors, and cable termination
  • Hard drive (some quotes mention NVR but no hard drive)
  • Remote access configuration
  • User training

Beware of Very Cheap Quotes

If someone quotes you a 4-camera CCTV system for KSh 8,000–12,000 installed, be very cautious. This likely means counterfeit cameras, no hard drive, no warranty, and/or no power supply. The cheapest option often costs more in the long run due to early failure and repeat installation costs.

How to Get the Best Value CCTV System in Kenya

  1. Get a free site survey first: A reputable company will visit your property to understand your specific needs before quoting
  2. Get at least 3 quotes: Compare itemised quotations, not just the total
  3. Ask for camera model numbers: Research the specific cameras being quoted to verify they're genuine
  4. Check for a warranty in writing: Any professional installer will put their warranty commitment on paper
  5. Read Google reviews: Check the installer's online reputation before committing
